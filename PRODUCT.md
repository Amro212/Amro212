# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters, hiring managers, and technical interviewers evaluating Amro Abedmoosa for new grad software engineering, AI automation, full-stack, frontend, backend, embedded/systems, and technical analyst roles.

## Product Purpose

This portfolio positions Amro as a practical Computer Engineering builder who turns messy real-world inputs into structured, useful software systems. It should make his range legible without diluting the strongest story: AI-assisted automation, product-minded software, frontend polish, performance benchmarking, embedded depth, and professional IT/platform experience.

## Brand Personality

Technical, precise, product-minded, quietly ambitious.

## Anti-references

Cheap neon cards, gimmicky pixel motion, exaggerated skill claims, generic developer portfolio grids, overbuilt sci-fi dashboards, and any visual treatment that makes the work feel less credible than the underlying experience.

## Design Principles

- Show builder range as a connected operating system, not a loose list of tools.
- Make professional credibility the first impression; let retro character support the story without becoming the story.
- Favor concrete capability groups over vague skill badges.
- Keep motion measured and purposeful, with content readable before animation runs.
- Never overstate incomplete projects or inflate academic work into production claims.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA for contrast, readable text, focus visibility, and responsive behavior. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` by keeping content visible and removing pinned or choreographed animation for motion-sensitive users.
